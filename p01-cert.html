Phase 01 Certification

Covers Lesson 1.1 (The Commercial Ecosystem — Stakeholder Map) and Lesson 1.2 (Commercial Rights & How Value Flows). An employee cannot begin Phase 02 until they pass this certification.

This is a cumulative, higher-stakes checkpoint — not a repeat of the lesson quizzes. It exists to confirm the employee can operate the foundational skill of the entire academy without prompting: given an unfamiliar organization, map the full ecosystem, identify the commercial rights in play, and reconstruct the likely money flow, then explain all of it to a skeptical listener in plain language.

Knowledge Assessment

(Manager-administered, closed-notes, written. Time limit: 30 minutes. Answer key follows each question in italics — remove before administering.)

Section A — Recall (5 questions, 2 points each = 10 points)

  1. Name all six core roles in the commercial ecosystem. Rights Holder, Brand, Commercial Rights Company, Media, Fans, Secondary Stakeholders (agencies, venues, ticketing, digital platforms).

  2. Name all eight major categories of commercial rights. Naming Rights, Media Rights, Sponsorship/Partnership Rights, Signage & Venue Rights, Digital & Social Rights, Hospitality & Premium Rights, Licensing Rights, Data & CRM Rights.

  3. What are the three things that make something a commercial right rather than a vague opportunity? Ownable, grantable, boundable.

  4. Name VYRO's three possible compensation models. Flat management fee, sales commission on rights fees generated, revenue share.

  5. What is the correct sequence for moving from a raw commercial right to a priced offer? Right → Value → Package → Price.

Section B — Applied Reasoning (3 questions, 10 points each = 30 points)

  1. A regional grocery chain wants to sponsor a mid-major university's Olympic sports programs (not football or basketball). Identify at least four ecosystem stakeholders you would need to investigate before drafting a proposal, and explain what you'd need to learn from each. Expected: names 4+ of Rights Holder (existing commercial representation), Brand (grocery chain's actual business objective and category exclusivity conflicts), any existing Commercial Rights Company, Media (any streaming/broadcast of these sports), Agencies (brand-side or university-side), Venue ownership if relevant. Each paired with a specific, non-generic question to investigate.

  2. Explain, in your own words, why VYRO's compensation is "earned, not extracted." Your answer must include what VYRO invests before any revenue exists. Expected: covers VYRO's upfront investment (audit, valuation, packaging) done before revenue exists, and that compensation is tied to generating revenue the Rights Holder could not generate independently.

  3. A Brand pays $750,000 over three years for a bundle of sponsorship, digital, and hospitality rights. VYRO's agreement specifies a 15% commission. Calculate what the Rights Holder nets annually (assume even distribution across the three years) and explain, in two sentences, how you would present this to the client. Expected: $750,000 − 15% ($112,500) = $637,500 total / 3 years = $212,500/year net to Rights Holder. Explanation should frame this as new, incremental revenue and should not apologize for or minimize VYRO's commission.

Section C — Synthesis (1 question, 20 points)

  1. In one paragraph (150–250 words), explain what VYRO does and why it exists, as if speaking to a rights holder's board of directors who have never heard of VYRO or the commercial rights industry. Your explanation must reference at least four of the six ecosystem roles and must correctly characterize VYRO as a managed relationship, not a transactional broker. Graded qualitatively against the Grading Rubric below — this is the single most important answer on the assessment, because it is the actual skill (explaining VYRO credibly, cold) that Phase 01 exists to build.

Total: 60 points. Minimum passing score for Knowledge Assessment: 48/60 (80%).

Practical Assessment

Exercise: Full Ecosystem & Money Flow Teardown

Manager assigns one unfamiliar organization the employee has not previously researched — not used in any Phase 01 homework. Suggested categories: a professional sports team, a music festival, a motorsports series, a convention center, or a university athletic department not already covered.

Time limit: 45 minutes, closed-collaboration (no asking colleagues), open internet research permitted (this mirrors real VYRO prospecting work).

Deliverables:

  1. A complete Six-Party Stakeholder Map (Lesson 1.1 template).
  2. A complete Money Flow Ledger (Lesson 1.2 template), with reasonable estimates clearly labeled where figures aren't public.
  3. A written, one-paragraph "board-ready" explanation of what a VYRO engagement with this organization could look like, following the same standard as Knowledge Assessment Question 9.

Verbal component: A 5-minute walkthrough of all three deliverables to the manager, followed by 5 minutes of manager questions/pushback (manager should ask at least one skeptical question about VYRO's fee, modeled on the Lesson 1.2 role-play).

Evaluation focus: completeness and accuracy of both maps, quality of reasoning where information had to be inferred, and composure/clarity during the verbal walkthrough and pushback.

Manager Evaluation

Manager completes this evaluation based on direct observation across both lessons' homework, quizzes, practical exercises, role-plays, and the Phase 01 Practical Assessment above. This is a qualitative sign-off in addition to the numeric scores — a manager can hold back certification even with passing scores if they have specific, documented concerns.

Evaluation criteria:

  • Employee produces complete, accurate stakeholder maps without prompting for missing roles
  • Employee produces complete, accurate money flow ledgers, correctly placing VYRO's compensation
  • Employee explains VYRO's business model and compensation confidently, with no hedging, in both written and verbal form
  • Employee correctly distinguishes Rights Holder / Brand / Commercial Rights Company under time pressure
  • Employee frames every deal in relationship terms (multi-year), not transaction terms (one-time sale)
  • Employee's written work is professional, well-organized, and free of generic filler language (e.g., "passionate fanbase")
  • Employee handled skeptical pushback (practical assessment verbal component) by reframing toward value, not becoming defensive

Minimum Passing Score

Certification requires all three of the following:

Component Weight Minimum to Pass
Knowledge Assessment 40% 48/60 (80%)
Practical Assessment (manager-scored against Lesson 1.1 §20 and Lesson 1.2 §20 rubrics, averaged) 40% 80/100 (80%)
Manager Evaluation 20% All 7 criteria checked, no unresolved concerns

An employee who fails any single component does not advance, even if their composite average would otherwise pass. This is intentional — Phase 01 is foundational, and a gap in any one area (ecosystem mapping, money flow, or professional communication) compounds through every later phase.

Remediation path: An employee who does not pass retakes the failed component(s) only, not the full phase, after a minimum of 48 hours of additional independent study and manager coaching. There is no limit on remediation attempts, but repeated failure on the same component should trigger a direct manager conversation about role fit before a third attempt.

Certification Checklist

Manager confirms each item before marking Phase 01 complete in the employee's record:

  • Lesson 1.1 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100
  • Lesson 1.1 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 1.1 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Lesson 1.2 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100
  • Lesson 1.2 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 1.2 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Phase 01 Knowledge Assessment passed (≥48/60)
  • Phase 01 Practical Assessment passed (≥80/100 average)
  • Phase 01 Manager Evaluation completed with all criteria checked
  • Employee's university capstone dry-run materials (stakeholder map + money flow ledger, from both lessons' homework) are complete and filed — these will be referenced again in the Phase 16 Capstone
  • Manager sign-off recorded with date

Once every box is checked, the employee is certified in Phase 01 and cleared to begin Phase 02 — The Language of Commercial Partnerships.
