Lesson 10.2 — Renewal, Upselling & Issue Resolution

1. Lesson Overview

Lesson 10.1 built the operational foundation of account management: handoffs, QBRs, reporting rhythm, and objective-aware performance review. This lesson builds the strategic layer on top of it — renewal planning, upselling, issue resolution, and the long-term relationship-building mindset that turns a single-term deal into a multi-year, compounding partnership.

This lesson produces this phase's core homework deliverable: a complete quarterly sponsor performance report with specific, well-reasoned recommendations for renewal — directly built on Lesson 10.1's QBR and performance review foundation, and representing the actual document a real VYRO account manager would produce and present to a client.

2. Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  1. Build a renewal timeline that begins well before a partnership term ends, using QBR and performance review history as its evidence base.
  2. Identify genuine upsell signals and propose a specific, well-justified tier upgrade or expansion.
  3. Apply a structured Issue Resolution Protocol to a real relationship problem, distinct from but complementary to Phase 04's sales objection framework.
  4. Explain the cumulative practices that build genuine long-term client relationships, connecting directly back to Phase 01's founding principle.
  5. Produce a complete quarterly sponsor performance report with a specific, defensible renewal recommendation.

3. Business Context

Renewal is not a single conversation that happens at the end of a term — it's the cumulative result of everything done throughout the relationship: strong activation (Phase 09), disciplined reporting (Phase 09, Lesson 10.1), genuine QBRs (Lesson 10.1), and proactive issue resolution. An account manager who waits until 30 days before term-end to start thinking about renewal is already behind. This lesson teaches renewal, upselling, and issue resolution as ongoing disciplines woven throughout the account management relationship, not as a separate, late-stage sales push.

4. Why This Matters

  • A renewal case built only at the last minute is weaker than one built cumulatively throughout the term, using real QBR and reporting history as evidence.
  • Upselling that isn't grounded in genuine client need reads as opportunistic, damaging trust — but upselling that responds to a real, demonstrated signal strengthens the relationship.
  • Unresolved issues compound. A small problem addressed early and well often strengthens trust; the same problem left to fester until renewal time can sink an otherwise strong relationship.
  • Long-term relationships are VYRO's actual business model (Phase 01) — this lesson is where that principle becomes a concrete, practiced discipline rather than an abstract value statement.

5. Core Lesson

5.1 Renewal Planning

Renewal planning should begin well before a partnership term ends — a useful default timeline: begin informal renewal-oriented conversation at roughly 90 days before term end, present a formal renewal proposal around 60 days out, and aim for a decision by 30 days out, leaving adequate runway for negotiation and activation planning for the next term. The renewal case itself should draw directly on the cumulative evidence built throughout the term: QBR outcomes, performance reviews, and end-of-term reporting (Lesson 10.1, Phase 09) — not be constructed from scratch at the last minute.

5.2 Upselling

Upselling means proposing a genuine expansion of the relationship — a tier upgrade (per Phase 08's Ladder) or the addition of new asset categories not originally included — grounded in a real, demonstrated signal rather than a generic sales push. Strong upsell signals include: a client consistently engaging beyond what their current tier requires, a business objective evolving in a way the current package no longer fully serves, or measurable results strong enough to justify expanded investment. An upsell proposal should reference the specific signal that prompted it, directly connecting back to real account management evidence, not simply proposing "more" for its own sake.

5.3 Issue Resolution

Relationship problems inevitably arise over a multi-quarter or multi-year term — underperforming activation, a changed internal stakeholder, a miscommunication about deliverables. Apply a structured Issue Resolution Protocol: Acknowledge the issue directly and promptly (never let a known problem go unaddressed), Investigate the actual root cause (not just the surface complaint), Resolve with a specific, concrete action plan, and Follow Up to confirm the resolution actually worked. This protocol is distinct from but complementary to Phase 04's A-C-R-C sales objection framework — issue resolution addresses problems within an existing relationship, not objections during a sale.

5.4 Long-Term Relationship Building

Long-term relationships are built cumulatively through consistent execution of everything in Phases 09 and 10: reliable activation delivery, honest and disciplined reporting, genuine strategic engagement in QBRs, well-timed and well-justified upselling, and prompt, thorough issue resolution. No single action creates a long-term relationship — it's the compounding effect of consistently doing all of this well, term after term, that produces the multi-year client relationships that are the actual foundation of VYRO's business model, as established in Phase 01.

6. Commercial Frameworks

Framework: The Renewal Timeline

T-90 DAYS: Begin informal renewal-oriented conversation, drawing on QBR/reporting history
        ↓
T-60 DAYS: Present formal renewal proposal
        ↓
T-30 DAYS: Target decision, leaving runway for negotiation and next-term activation planning

Framework: Upsell Signal Checklist

  • Client engagement consistently exceeds current tier's baseline expectations
  • Business objective has evolved in a way the current package doesn't fully serve
  • Measured results (Phase 09) are strong enough to justify expanded investment
  • A specific, real signal (not a generic assumption) can be cited as the basis for the upsell proposal

Framework: The Issue Resolution Protocol (A-I-R-F)

ACKNOWLEDGE (promptly, directly)
        ↓
INVESTIGATE (actual root cause, not just surface complaint)
        ↓
RESOLVE (specific, concrete action plan)
        ↓
FOLLOW UP (confirm the resolution actually worked)

7. Step-by-Step Process

How to build a complete renewal case:

  1. Beginning at T-90 days, compile the cumulative QBR outcomes and performance reviews from Lesson 10.1 across the full term.
  2. Assemble the final comprehensive end-of-term report (Phase 09's structure), incorporating all measured performance data.
  3. Identify any genuine upsell signal (Section 6 checklist) and, if present, incorporate a specific expansion proposal into the renewal case.
  4. Confirm any issues raised during the term were fully resolved (via the A-I-R-F protocol) and, where relevant, reference the resolution as evidence of relationship strength.
  5. Present the formal renewal proposal at T-60 days, with a clear, evidence-based recommendation.
  6. Target a decision by T-30 days, allowing adequate transition time into the next term.

8. Real Industry Examples

A renewal proposal that references specific, cumulative QBR outcomes across the term ("in Q2 we identified X, and by Q3 we had resolved it, resulting in Y improvement") rather than only presenting a final snapshot — illustrates Section 5.1's cumulative renewal case-building in practice.

An upsell proposal for expanded digital rights, prompted specifically by a brand's own marketing team increasing organic engagement with the property's content beyond contractual requirements — illustrates Section 5.2's genuine-signal-based upselling.

A hospitality utilization issue identified in a mid-term report (Phase 09), acknowledged promptly, investigated to find a scheduling-coordination root cause, resolved with a new booking process, and confirmed improved in the following quarter's report — illustrates the full Issue Resolution Protocol in sequence.

9. VYRO Best Practices

  • Begin renewal planning at T-90 days, not T-30 — a rushed, last-minute renewal case is measurably weaker than a cumulative one.
  • Always ground upsell proposals in a specific, real signal — never propose expansion simply because it would generate more revenue without a genuine client-side rationale.
  • Acknowledge issues promptly, even when the full resolution isn't yet ready — silence on a known problem damages trust faster than the problem itself.
  • Always close the loop with a Follow Up step after resolving an issue — an unconfirmed resolution risks the same problem recurring unnoticed.
  • Treat every well-handled quarter as a building block toward a multi-year relationship, not as an isolated, self-contained success.

10. Common Mistakes

  • Waiting until the final weeks of a term to begin renewal planning.
  • Proposing an upsell without a genuine, specific signal, reading as opportunistic rather than client-focused.
  • Acknowledging an issue but skipping the Investigate step, resolving the surface complaint without addressing its actual root cause.
  • Skipping the Follow Up step, leaving a resolution unconfirmed and vulnerable to recurrence.
  • Treating long-term relationship building as a mindset rather than a set of specific, repeatable practices.

11. Professional Tips

  • Keep a running renewal-evidence file throughout the term (QBR notes, performance reviews, resolved issues) so the T-90-day renewal case-building process starts from a rich foundation rather than a blank page.
  • When considering an upsell, write out the specific signal in one sentence before drafting the proposal — if you can't state the signal clearly, the proposal likely isn't ready.
  • Practice the Issue Resolution Protocol on smaller, lower-stakes issues as they arise, so the habit is already strong before a genuinely significant problem occurs.

  • Renewal case built cumulatively from real QBR and performance review history, not assembled last-minute
  • Any upsell proposal grounded in a specific, real signal

14. Worksheets

Worksheet: Upsell Signal Audit

Review your Phase 09/10.1 partner's activation and QBR history (real or realistically constructed) and identify whether a genuine upsell signal exists. If yes, specify it and draft a one-paragraph upsell rationale. If no, explain why an upsell would be premature or unjustified at this time — this is an equally valid and important conclusion.

15. Homework

  1. Complete the Upsell Signal Audit worksheet.
  2. Write a complete Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report for your Phase 09/10.1 partner, using the Phase 02 Exposure/Experience/Proof structure, populated with realistic data consistent with your prior homework.
  3. Include a specific, well-reasoned renewal recommendation within that report — this satisfies the original academy requirement to produce a quarterly sponsor performance report with recommendations for renewal.
  4. Draft one complete Issue Resolution Log for a realistic problem that could plausibly arise during this partnership's term, carrying it through all four A-I-R-F steps.

Deliverable format: one document containing the Upsell Signal Audit, the complete Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report with renewal recommendation, and the Issue Resolution Log.

16. Manager Coaching Notes

Purpose of this lesson in the employee's development: This lesson produces one of the most directly client-usable deliverables in the entire academy. Managers should evaluate the Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report and renewal recommendation with the same standard as a real client-facing document, and should specifically probe whether the renewal recommendation is genuinely evidence-based rather than a generic "we recommend renewal" conclusion.

Discussion questions to ask in review:

  • "Walk me through the specific evidence in your report that supports your renewal recommendation."
  • "You identified [an upsell signal, or the absence of one] — walk me through your reasoning."
  • "Pick your Issue Resolution Log — what would you have done differently if the Investigate step had revealed a different root cause?"

Expected answers: A proficient employee can trace their renewal recommendation directly to specific evidence in the report (not just assert it), can defend their upsell conclusion (whether positive or negative) with real reasoning, and demonstrates genuine problem-solving flexibility in the Issue Resolution Log discussion.

Coaching tips:

  • If the renewal recommendation reads as generic or unsupported by the report's own data, send it back — the connection between evidence and recommendation is the core skill being tested.
  • If the Upsell Signal Audit concludes "yes" without a genuinely specific signal, or "no" without real reasoning, push the employee to sharpen their justification either way.

Common employee mistakes to watch for:

  • Generic renewal recommendations disconnected from the report's actual evidence.
  • Upsell conclusions based on convenience rather than genuine signal.
  • Issue Resolution Logs that skip the Investigate step and jump straight to a surface-level fix.

Evaluation checklist: see Grading Rubric (Section 20).

18. Practical Exercise

Exercise: Renewal Case Defense

Manager provides a summary of cumulative QBR/reporting history for an unfamiliar partnership, including at least one resolved issue and one ambiguous upsell signal. Employee has 30 minutes to build a complete renewal case (using the Renewal Case Builder), including a clear recommendation on the ambiguous upsell signal.

Evaluation focus: whether the renewal recommendation is directly evidence-based, and whether the upsell judgment (yes or no) is well-reasoned rather than default-positive or default-cautious.

19. Role-Play Exercise

Scenario: You're presenting a renewal proposal that includes an upsell recommendation. Your manager plays the client, who is open to renewal but skeptical of the proposed upsell.

Manager's role-play brief: Say, "I'm fine renewing at our current level, but I'm not sure why we'd need to upgrade — convince me." Push for the specific signal behind the recommendation, not just enthusiasm for the idea.

What "good" looks like: The employee references the specific, real signal that prompted the upsell recommendation (not a generic sales pitch), and is willing to accept a renewal at the current tier gracefully if the client remains unconvinced, rather than pushing the upsell aggressively against genuine resistance.

23. Key Takeaways

  • Renewal planning should begin at roughly T-90 days and build cumulatively from real QBR and performance review evidence, not be assembled at the last minute.
  • Upselling should always be grounded in a specific, genuine signal — never proposed simply because it would generate more revenue.
  • The Issue Resolution Protocol (Acknowledge, Investigate, Resolve, Follow Up) ensures problems are actually fixed at their root cause, not just superficially addressed.
  • Long-term relationships are the compounding result of consistently strong activation, reporting, QBRs, upselling, and issue resolution — not any single action.
  • This lesson's Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report with a specific renewal recommendation is the tangible, client-ready product of everything taught across Phase 10 — and, cumulatively, everything taught since Phase 01.
