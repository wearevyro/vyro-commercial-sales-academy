Phase 07 Certification

Covers Lesson 7.1 (Audience & Reach Valuation Inputs) and Lesson 7.2 (Market-Based Valuation & Pricing). An employee cannot begin Phase 08 until they pass this certification.

Phase 07 certifies whether an employee can produce a full, defensible valuation from raw data through to a final price range — and defend every number in it under pressure from both directions simultaneously. Valuation sits at the exact point where a rights holder's desire for a higher price and a brand's desire for a lower one both bear down on the same figure; this certification's practical component is built specifically to test composure and reasoning under that two-sided pressure.

Knowledge Assessment

(Manager-administered, closed-notes, written. Time limit: 35 minutes. Answer key follows each question in italics — remove before administering.)

Section A — Recall (6 questions, 2 points each = 12 points)

  1. Name the six audience/reach valuation inputs from Lesson 7.1. Audience Size, Attendance, Broadcast Reach, Social Reach, Fan Engagement, Media Value.

  2. Name the five market-based/qualitative factors from Lesson 7.2. Comparable Deals, Scarcity, Brand Fit, Category Demand, Exclusivity.

  3. What are the three components of a correctly structured final price? Floor, Anchor, Ceiling.

  4. Why should Fan Engagement modify raw reach figures rather than being reported separately and ignored? Because two properties with identical reach can have meaningfully different real value depending on audience engagement quality — engagement is a quality modifier, not a side note.

  5. Why must comparable deals always be adjusted rather than used directly? Because real differences in scale, rights bundle, and timing mean a raw comparable price rarely transfers directly to a new, different asset.

  6. Why should the exclusivity premium be stated as a distinct line item? So the valuation stays transparent and defensible, and the premium can be specifically discussed, negotiated, or removed independent of the base asset value.

Section B — Applied Valuation (24 points)

  1. Manager provides a brief profile of an unfamiliar asset (type, basic audience/reach data, and two comparable deals). In writing, build a complete Valuation Input Stack and Valuation Synthesis Worksheet, producing a full Floor–Anchor–Ceiling range with documented assumptions. Graded against Lesson 7.1 §20 and Lesson 7.2 §20 rubric criteria for Business Understanding, Commercial Strategy, and Accuracy.

Section C — Two-Sided Defense (24 points)

  1. Part A (12 points): Manager (as a rights holder) argues the anchor price from Question 7 is too low. Employee responds in writing, referencing specific valuation factors. Part B (12 points): Manager (as a brand) argues the same anchor price is too high. Employee responds in writing, referencing specific valuation factors — the two written responses must be internally consistent with each other and with the original valuation, not contradictory. Scoring: full points require specific, factor-based reasoning in both directions that remains logically consistent with the original Floor–Anchor–Ceiling range — a response that simply moves the number to appease whichever side is currently pushing, without grounding in the stated methodology, should be scored as Developing or below regardless of how persuasive it sounds in isolation.

Total: 60 points. Minimum passing score for Knowledge Assessment: 48/60 (80%).

Practical Assessment

Exercise: Live Two-Sided Valuation Defense

Manager assigns one unfamiliar asset with basic supporting data and at least two real or realistic comparable deals.

Time limit: 30 minutes to build, followed by a 20-minute live defense.

Deliverables (submitted before the live defense):

  1. A complete Valuation Input Stack (Lesson 7.1).
  2. A complete Valuation Synthesis Worksheet with Floor–Anchor–Ceiling range and documented methodology (Lesson 7.2).

Live defense structure:

  1. Rights holder pressure (7 minutes) — manager plays a rights holder pushing for a higher anchor, referencing at least one real or plausible reason (e.g., "a nearby comparable market got significantly more").
  2. Brand pressure (7 minutes) — manager plays a brand pushing for a lower anchor or a discount, referencing at least one real or plausible reason (e.g., "this doesn't have the exclusivity we'd need to justify that number").
  3. Consistency check (6 minutes) — manager directly asks the employee to reconcile their two responses: "you told the rights holder X and the brand Y — are those actually consistent with each other?"

Evaluation focus: whether the underlying valuation methodology holds up under pressure from both directions without becoming internally contradictory, and whether the employee can clearly explain why the anchor sits where it does rather than simply defending a number reflexively.

Manager Evaluation

Evaluation criteria:

  • Employee's Valuation Input Stacks (Lesson 7.1 homework) correctly select inputs by asset type, not a generic full-input approach applied uniformly
  • Employee's five final valuations (Lesson 7.2 homework) all include properly adjusted comparable deals, not direct copy-paste pricing
  • Exclusivity premiums are consistently quantified as distinct line items across all homework and practical work
  • Employee's Floor–Anchor–Ceiling ranges are realistic and well-reasoned, not arbitrarily rounded
  • Employee defends the anchor price credibly under rights holder-side pressure without simply capitulating to a higher number
  • Employee defends the anchor price credibly under brand-side pressure without simply capitulating to a lower number
  • Employee's two directional defenses remain internally consistent with each other and with the original stated methodology

Minimum Passing Score

Certification requires all three of the following:

Component Weight Minimum to Pass
Knowledge Assessment 35% 48/60 (80%)
Practical Assessment — Live Two-Sided Valuation Defense (manager-scored against Lesson 7.1 §20 and Lesson 7.2 §20 rubrics, averaged) 45% 80/100 (80%)
Manager Evaluation 20% All 7 criteria checked, no unresolved concerns

The consistency requirement is the distinguishing feature of this certification. An employee who successfully appeases the rights holder in one moment and the brand in the next, but does so by contradicting their own stated methodology, has not demonstrated the actual skill this phase teaches — real valuation discipline holds its shape under pressure from both sides simultaneously. Manager judgment on this point should weigh heavily, even where the Knowledge Assessment score is strong. As in every prior phase, failing any single component blocks advancement regardless of composite average.

Remediation path: Standard remediation (48 hours, unlimited attempts, role-fit conversation after a third failure) applies. For the Practical Assessment specifically, remediation must include at least one additional full live two-sided defense before the retake — a written-only remediation is not sufficient, since the skill being certified is real-time consistency under pressure.

Certification Checklist

  • Lesson 7.1 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Input Relevance Practice + 5 Valuation Input Stacks)
  • Lesson 7.1 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 7.1 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Lesson 7.2 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Comparable Deal Adjustment Drill + 5 completed Valuation Synthesis Worksheets + methodology paragraphs)
  • Lesson 7.2 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 7.2 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • All five asset valuations (Stadium Naming Rights, Jersey Sponsorship, Official Bank, Official Airline, Official Beverage Partner) complete with Floor–Anchor–Ceiling ranges and documented methodology
  • Phase 07 Knowledge Assessment passed (≥48/60)
  • Phase 07 Practical Assessment — Live Two-Sided Valuation Defense — passed (≥80/100 average), including a consistent, non-contradictory defense in both directions
  • Phase 07 Manager Evaluation completed with all criteria checked
  • Manager sign-off recorded with date

Once every box is checked, the employee is certified in Phase 07 and cleared to begin Phase 08 — Sponsorship Packages.
