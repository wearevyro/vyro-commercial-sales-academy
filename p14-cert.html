Phase 14 Certification

Covers Lesson 14.1 (Foundational Identity & Structure) and Lesson 14.2 (Operating Systems & Business Blueprint). An employee cannot begin Phase 15 until they pass this certification.

Phase 14 certifies the single most comprehensive synthesis skill in the academy: the ability to look back across everything taught since Phase 01 and produce one coherent, genuinely usable company design. The practical assessment is deliberately built around a "new hire test" — the blueprint has to function as a real reference document under specific, practical questioning, not just read well as a completed assignment.

Knowledge Assessment

(Manager-administered, closed-notes, written. Time limit: 30 minutes. Answer key follows each question in italics — remove before administering.)

Section A — Recall (6 questions, 2 points each = 12 points)

  1. Distinguish mission from vision. Mission describes present purpose (what we do now); vision describes future aspiration (where we're headed).

  2. What test should every stated core value pass? The Values-to-Practice Traceability test — tracing to a specific, real practice taught in the academy, not generic language.

  3. Name the seven components of the Operating Systems Stack. Pricing Strategy, CRM Process, Sales Process, Proposal Template Library, Website Structure, Operations Manual, Brand Guidelines.

  4. Why should the proposal template library be built from real prior academy work rather than generic outside sources? Because the academy has already produced tested, VYRO-specific templates directly connected to real practice — generic templates lack that grounding and consistency.

  5. What should an operations manual's escalation paths connect back to? Phase 09's compliance escalation and Phase 10's issue resolution discipline.

  6. Why does organizational structure need consistent underlying functional logic across all three scaling stages? Because the core functions (prospecting/closing, relationship management, activation) remain necessary regardless of size — only specialization degree changes, and consistent logic prevents Phase 11's coordination failures.

Section B — Applied Blueprint Section (24 points)

  1. Manager assigns one specific blueprint section not previously seen by the employee (e.g., "draft the CRM process section" or "draft the brand guidelines section with examples"). In writing, produce a complete, specific version of that section, correctly cross-referenced to the relevant earlier phase(s). Graded against Lesson 14.1 §20 and Lesson 14.2 §20 rubric criteria for Business Understanding and Accuracy.

Section C — New Hire Test (24 points)

  1. Manager poses four specific, practical questions a genuine new hire would ask (e.g., "where do I find the valuation template," "what's our escalation path for an unresolved client issue," "what's one of our core values and how do I know it's real," "what's step one of our sales process"). Answer each in writing, citing the specific blueprint section that answers it. Scoring: 6 points per question; full credit requires a specific, correct answer with the correct section citation — "it's in the document" or a vague paraphrase does not receive credit.

Total: 60 points. Minimum passing score for Knowledge Assessment: 48/60 (80%).

Practical Assessment

Exercise: Full Blueprint Defense — New Hire Onboarding Simulation

Manager reviews the employee's complete submitted VYRO Business Blueprint (Lesson 14.1 + 14.2 homework) in advance.

Time limit: 20-minute live session (no additional build time — this tests the blueprint as already submitted).

Live session structure:

  1. New hire onboarding walkthrough (10 minutes) — manager plays a brand-new VYRO employee on day one, asking a rapid series of specific, practical questions drawn from across all eight blueprint sections (not disclosed in advance). Employee must answer each specifically and confidently, referencing their own document.
  2. Traceability spot-check (5 minutes) — manager picks two core values at random and requires the employee to trace each to a specific practice and phase, live.
  3. Coherence challenge (5 minutes) — manager asks the employee to explain how two specific, manager-chosen sections of the Operating Systems Stack depend on each other (e.g., "how does your pricing strategy actually govern your sales process, concretely?").

Evaluation focus: whether the blueprint functions as a genuinely usable reference under real, specific questioning — not whether it reads well as a static document.

Manager Evaluation

Evaluation criteria:

  • Employee's Foundational Identity Document (Lesson 14.1) has fully traceable core values, with no generic or unsupported claims
  • Employee's Operating Systems Stack (Lesson 14.2) sections are internally consistent and correctly cross-referenced to source phases
  • Employee's proposal template library is genuinely built from real prior academy work, not generic placeholders
  • Employee answers new-hire-style practical questions specifically and confidently, citing the correct blueprint section every time
  • Employee demonstrates the Operating Systems Stack's layered dependencies concretely, not just by naming the framework
  • Employee's organizational structure and service catalog are complete, well-organized, and consistent with Phase 11's functional logic
  • The complete Business Blueprint reads as genuinely usable company documentation, not a training-exercise artifact

Minimum Passing Score

Certification requires all three of the following:

Component Weight Minimum to Pass
Knowledge Assessment 30% 48/60 (80%)
Practical Assessment — Full Blueprint Defense (manager-scored against Lesson 14.1 §20 and Lesson 14.2 §20 rubrics, averaged) 50% 80/100 (80%)
Manager Evaluation 20% All 7 criteria checked, no unresolved concerns

A blueprint that reads well but fails the live New Hire Onboarding Simulation has not passed this certification, regardless of how polished the written document appears — the entire point of Phase 14 is producing something genuinely usable, and usability can only be confirmed under live, specific questioning. As in every prior phase, failing any single component blocks advancement regardless of composite average.

Remediation path: Standard remediation (48 hours, unlimited attempts, role-fit conversation after a third failure) applies. For the Practical Assessment, remediation must include revising the specific blueprint sections that failed the live test, followed by a fresh live session with different new-hire questions — not a repeat of the same question set.

Certification Checklist

  • Lesson 14.1 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Values Traceability Audit + Foundational Identity Document)
  • Lesson 14.1 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 14.1 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Lesson 14.2 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Template Library Audit + complete VYRO Business Blueprint)
  • Lesson 14.2 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 14.2 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Complete VYRO Business Blueprint verified as genuinely usable (manager spot-check via new-hire-style questioning)
  • Phase 14 Knowledge Assessment passed (≥48/60)
  • Phase 14 Practical Assessment — Full Blueprint Defense — passed (≥80/100 average)
  • Phase 14 Manager Evaluation completed with all criteria checked
  • Manager sign-off recorded with date

Once every box is checked, the employee is certified in Phase 14 and cleared to begin Phase 15 — Executive Leadership.
