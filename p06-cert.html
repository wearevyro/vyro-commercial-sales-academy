Phase 06 Certification

Covers Lesson 6.1 (Physical & Rights-Based Assets) and Lesson 6.2 (Digital, Experience & Community Assets). An employee cannot begin Phase 07 until they pass this certification.

Phase 06 certifies two things simultaneously: thoroughness (the ability to systematically discover a rights holder's full commercial inventory without stopping at the obvious assets) and judgment (the ability to correctly draw the fan data compliance line, every time, without exception). Unlike every prior certification in this academy, this one has a component that is treated as a near-mandatory gate rather than a weighted score — fan data compliance.

Knowledge Assessment

(Manager-administered, closed-notes, written. Time limit: 30 minutes. Answer key follows each question in italics — remove before administering.)

Section A — Recall (5 questions, 2 points each = 10 points)

  1. Name the ten zones of the full (combined) Asset Audit Walkthrough. Exterior, Concourse, Seating Bowl, Playing Surface/Court/Field, Training & Locker Facilities, Broadcast & Digital, Merchandise & Licensing (Lesson 6.1), plus Digital & Owned Channels, Access & Experience, Community & Relationship (Lesson 6.2).

  2. Define the Rights Layer Stack and give an example. The multiple distinct commercial rights (naming, media, digital, licensing) that can attach to a single physical asset simultaneously — e.g., a stadium naming rights deal that also carries digital-channel exposure, broadcast mention value, and merchandise appearance.

  3. Define the Countable Unit Conversion and give an example. Converting a vague asset description into a specific quantity, frequency, and availability — e.g., "some hospitality access" becomes "6 suite tickets per home game across an 18-game season."

  4. State the three zones of the Fan Data Compliance Boundary. Green (generally offerable — aggregate/anonymized, opt-in), Yellow (requires legal/compliance review), Red (not offerable without explicit legal clearance and consent).

  5. Explain the distinction between rights holder-controlled player appearance inventory and individual athlete NIL rights. Appearance inventory (clinics, corporate events booked through the organization) is controlled by the rights holder within governing body rules; NIL/endorsement rights are personal to the athlete and not the rights holder's to sell.

Section B — Applied Audit (20 points)

  1. Manager provides a brief profile of an unfamiliar organization. In 15 minutes, identify at least 15 real, plausible assets spanning at least four different walkthrough zones, each with a Countable Unit Conversion where applicable. Graded against Lesson 6.1 §20 and Lesson 6.2 §20 rubric criteria for Research, Commercial Strategy, and Accuracy.

Section C — Fan Data Judgment (30 points — near-mandatory pass component)

  1. Manager presents six distinct fan-data scenarios in writing, ranging clearly Green to clearly Red, with at least two deliberately ambiguous (Yellow) cases. For each, zone the scenario and briefly justify the classification. 5 points per correct, well-justified zoning. Scoring note: this section is scored strictly. A single scenario incorrectly zoned as more permissive than it actually is (e.g., a Red scenario zoned Green) should result in an automatic Knowledge Assessment fail on this attempt, regardless of total points — see Minimum Passing Score below.

Total: 60 points. Minimum passing score for Knowledge Assessment: 48/60 (80%), AND zero under-permissive fan data misclassifications in Section C.

Practical Assessment

Exercise: Rapid Full-Spectrum Audit

Manager assigns one unfamiliar, real organization (not previously audited by the employee).

Time limit: 50 minutes.

Deliverables:

  1. A combined Asset Inventory Log spanning at least six of the ten walkthrough zones, with a minimum of 40 real, distinct assets identified (a compressed version of the full 150-asset homework standard, appropriate to the time limit).
  2. At least five major assets with fully documented Rights Layer Stacks.
  3. At least ten assets converted through the Countable Unit Conversion (no vague descriptions accepted).
  4. At least two Fan Data Compliance Checks, correctly zoned.

Verbal component: A 10-minute walkthrough to the manager, who should ask about at least one zone the employee appears to have covered thinly, and pose one live fan-data scenario for verbal zoning and justification.

Evaluation focus: breadth across zones (not concentration in only 1–2 easy categories), specificity of countable units, and — again — correctness of fan data judgment under live questioning.

Manager Evaluation

Evaluation criteria:

  • Employee's full 150+ asset homework inventory spans all ten walkthrough zones, not concentrated in a few easy categories
  • Rights Layer Stacks are consistently multi-layer where applicable, not defaulting to single-layer documentation
  • Experiential and community assets are consistently converted to specific countable units, not left vague
  • Employee correctly and conservatively zones every fan data scenario encountered, in both written and live formats, with zero under-permissive misclassifications
  • Employee clearly distinguishes rights holder-controlled appearance inventory from personal athlete NIL rights
  • Employee's Rapid Full-Spectrum Audit demonstrates real-time competence, not just prepared/rehearsed homework quality
  • Employee treats fan data questions with appropriate seriousness and escalation instinct, not casual improvisation

Minimum Passing Score

Certification requires all four of the following:

Component Weight Minimum to Pass
Knowledge Assessment 30% 48/60 (80%)
Practical Assessment — Rapid Full-Spectrum Audit (manager-scored against Lesson 6.1 §20 and Lesson 6.2 §20 rubrics, averaged) 40% 80/100 (80%)
Manager Evaluation 20% All 7 criteria checked, no unresolved concerns
Fan Data Compliance Gate Pass/Fail, not weighted Zero under-permissive fan data misclassifications, in both written and live components

The Fan Data Compliance Gate is a hard gate, not a weighted score component. An employee who scores well on every other component but incorrectly zones even one fan data scenario as more permissive than it actually is (in either the Knowledge Assessment or the live practical walkthrough) does not pass certification on that attempt, full stop — this mirrors the real-world stakes of the mistake, where a compliance error isn't a coaching opportunity but a real risk to VYRO and its clients. As with every prior phase, failing any other single component also blocks advancement regardless of composite average.

Remediation path: Standard remediation (48 hours, unlimited attempts, role-fit conversation after a third failure) applies to the Knowledge Assessment and Practical Assessment components. A Fan Data Compliance Gate failure requires a dedicated, separate remediation session with a manager specifically focused on data compliance judgment — not just a retake — before the employee is permitted to attempt certification again.

Certification Checklist

  • Lesson 6.1 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Practice Audit + 75+ physical/rights-based assets + 10 Rights Layer Stack write-ups)
  • Lesson 6.1 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 6.1 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Lesson 6.2 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Countable Unit Drill + extended inventory + 3 Fan Data Compliance Checks + final combined 150+ asset inventory)
  • Lesson 6.2 quiz passed (≥4/5), including correct answer on the compliance-focused applied question
  • Lesson 6.2 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Final combined asset inventory verified at 150+ distinct assets across all ten zones
  • Phase 06 Knowledge Assessment passed (≥48/60) with zero fan data misclassifications
  • Phase 06 Practical Assessment — Rapid Full-Spectrum Audit — passed (≥80/100 average)
  • Phase 06 Manager Evaluation completed with all criteria checked
  • Fan Data Compliance Gate passed cleanly (no separate remediation session pending)
  • Manager sign-off recorded with date

Once every box is checked, the employee is certified in Phase 06 and cleared to begin Phase 07 — Sponsorship Valuation.
