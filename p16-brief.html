Capstone Project Brief

Overview

Operate VYRO as the Commercial Rights Company for a university.

Every phase in this academy has built toward this moment. The Capstone is not a new set of skills — it is the integrated application of all fifteen preceding phases against one continuous, realistic client scenario, structured as five staged deliverables that build on each other in the same order the underlying skills were taught. There is no new content to learn in Phase 16. There is only the (considerable) work of doing everything you already know how to do, in sequence, at full professional standard, for one real-feeling client.

An employee who completes the Capstone has demonstrated they can operate independently as a VYRO Commercial Partnerships Professional — capable of taking on a real client relationship from first audit through executive-level strategic presentation, without needing to be taught any of the underlying skill along the way.

The University Client Scenario

A university hires VYRO to manage its complete commercial business. The university includes:

  • 20 varsity sports
  • A 15,000-seat football stadium
  • A basketball arena
  • A baseball stadium
  • A full athletic department
  • A digital streaming platform
  • An alumni network
  • Existing corporate partners

Treat this scenario as you would a real new client engagement. Where specific details aren't given, make reasonable, clearly-stated assumptions — consistent with how you've been trained throughout this academy to handle incomplete information (Phase 15's Executive Decision Framework applies directly here). Do not wait for information that isn't provided; assume what a real commercial professional would reasonably assume, state the assumption explicitly, and proceed.

This is the same university scenario introduced in Phase 01, Lesson 1.1, and referenced throughout the academy's homework (Phase 01's stakeholder map, Phase 02's money flow ledger, Phase 11's commercial strategy). If you completed those exercises with genuine care, you already have a running start on the Capstone — review your own prior work before starting fresh.

Your Responsibilities

Across the five stages of this Capstone, you will:

  1. Audit every commercial asset.
  2. Create a complete inventory.
  3. Value every asset.
  4. Build sponsorship packages.
  5. Identify 100 target companies.
  6. Prepare outreach campaigns.
  7. Conduct simulated discovery meetings.
  8. Negotiate simulated agreements.
  9. Develop activation plans.
  10. Produce sponsor performance reports.
  11. Recommend renewals and upsell opportunities.
  12. Present a five-year commercial growth strategy.

Every one of these twelve responsibilities maps directly to a stage below, and every stage maps directly back to specific earlier phases of this academy. Nothing here is unfamiliar — it is comprehensive.

Each stage produces a discrete, gated deliverable. As with every phase in this academy, stages are completed one at a time, reviewed, and approved before the next begins — but unlike earlier phases, each stage here is graded as a checkpoint, not a full certification cycle, because the terminal certification for the entire academy happens once, at the end of Stage 16.5, covering the complete integrated body of work.

Grading Philosophy

The Capstone is evaluated to full professional standard — the standard of real client-ready work, not training-exercise work. Every rubric, framework, and quality bar established across Phases 1–15 applies here in full: the Ladder Principle Test still applies to your packages, the Floor-Anchor-Ceiling discipline still applies to your valuations, the Fan Data Compliance Gate still applies if fan data comes up, the Executive Communication Pyramid still governs your final presentation. Nothing about the Capstone relaxes any standard already taught — it only asks you to hold all of them simultaneously, across one continuous body of work.

A manager reviewing Capstone work should feel, at every stage, that they are reviewing real work for a real client — because that is precisely the standard this academy exists to produce.

Timeline Guidance

Given the scope, the Capstone is expected to take meaningfully longer than any single prior phase — this is by design. Suggested pacing: allow at least as much time for each Capstone stage as was allowed for a full two-lesson phase earlier in the academy, with Stage 16.5 (the final strategic synthesis and live defense) allotted the most time of all, given its role as the terminal certification event for the entire program.

Stage 16.1 — Ecosystem & Asset Foundation

Overview

This stage covers Capstone Responsibilities 1 and 2: audit every commercial asset, and create a complete inventory. It maps directly to Phase 01 (ecosystem mapping), Phase 02 (terminology and rights classification), Phase 03 (case-study-informed pattern recognition), and Phase 06 (the full Asset Audit Walkthrough and Countable Unit Conversion discipline).

Objectives

By the end of this stage, you will have:

  1. A complete Six-Party Stakeholder Map for the university client (Phase 01).
  2. A complete Money Flow Ledger reflecting VYRO's engagement with this client (Phase 01).
  3. A full Rights Holder Archetype classification for the university, correctly identifying it as a federated/multi-institution structure with real reasoning (Phase 03).
  4. A complete Asset Inventory Log covering all ten walkthrough zones (Phase 06), reaching or exceeding 200 distinct commercial assets — a higher bar than Phase 06's 150-asset requirement, reflecting this client's larger, more complex asset base (20 varsity sports, three venues, a streaming platform, and an alumni network, versus the more general organization used in Phase 06's homework).
  5. Full Rights Layer Stack documentation for at least fifteen major assets.
  6. At least five plausible Fan Data Compliance Checks, correctly zoned, given the client's alumni network and streaming platform present real fan data touchpoints.

Deliverables Required

  • Six-Party Stakeholder Map (Phase 01 template)
  • Money Flow Ledger (Phase 01 template)
  • Rights Holder Archetype classification with written reasoning (Phase 03 framework)
  • Complete Asset Inventory Log, 200+ assets, organized by all ten Phase 06 zones
  • Fifteen Rights Layer Stack write-ups for the client's most significant assets
  • Five Fan Data Compliance Checks

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Build the Six-Party Stakeholder Map and Money Flow Ledger first — these establish the ecosystem context everything else depends on.
  2. Classify the university's rights holder archetype, reasoning explicitly through its federated structure (national association, conference, and institutional levels — Phase 03).
  3. Work the full Asset Audit Walkthrough across all ten zones (Phase 06), covering all 20 varsity sports, all three venues, the streaming platform, the alumni network, and existing corporate partner relationships.
  4. Apply the Countable Unit Conversion to every experiential, hospitality, and community asset identified — no vague descriptions.
  5. Document the Rights Layer Stack for the fifteen most significant assets (naming rights candidates, major signage, streaming platform sponsorship, etc.).
  6. Run every plausible fan-data-adjacent opportunity (particularly touching the alumni network and streaming platform) through the Fan Data Compliance Check, defaulting to caution.

Standards

This stage is graded against the combined rubrics of Phase 01 (Lessons 1.1–1.2), Phase 03 (Lesson 3.1), and Phase 06 (Lessons 6.1–6.2), applied at full standard. The Fan Data Compliance Gate from Phase 06's certification applies here in full: any under-permissive fan data misclassification blocks this stage's checkpoint outright, regardless of the quality of everything else submitted.

Manager Coaching Notes

Purpose of this stage in the employee's development: This is the first proof point that the employee can operate at full integrated scale — not because the individual skills are new, but because doing all of them, correctly, across a client this size, at once, is a genuinely different challenge than any single prior phase's homework.

Discussion questions to ask in review:

  • "This university's asset base is larger than anything you audited in Phase 06 — where did the extra scale actually come from? Which zones grew the most?"
  • "Walk me through your federated archetype reasoning — where specifically does authority split between the association, the conference, and the university itself?"
  • "Pick one of your Fan Data Compliance Checks involving the alumni network — walk me through your zoning."

Common employee mistakes to watch for:

  • Treating the 200-asset target as an extension of a single Phase 06 organization rather than genuinely re-auditing this specific, larger, multi-venue, multi-sport client.
  • Under-reasoning the federated archetype classification, treating the university as a simple single-entity rights holder.
  • Rushing fan data compliance checks given the higher volume of assets in this stage.

Stage Checkpoint

  • Six-Party Stakeholder Map complete
  • Money Flow Ledger complete
  • Rights Holder Archetype classified with full written reasoning
  • Asset Inventory Log reaches 200+ assets across all ten zones
  • Fifteen Rights Layer Stack write-ups complete
  • Five Fan Data Compliance Checks complete, all correctly zoned (hard gate — no under-permissive misclassifications)
  • Manager sign-off recorded

Once this checkpoint is complete, proceed to Stage 16.2 — Valuation & Packaging (draft pending your review of this stage).
