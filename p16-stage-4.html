Stage 16.4 — Activation & Client Management

Overview

This stage covers Capstone Responsibilities 9 through 11: develop activation plans, produce sponsor performance reports, and recommend renewals and upsell opportunities. It maps directly to Phase 09 (Partnership Activation) and Phase 10 (Client Management).

This stage picks up wherever Stage 16.3 left off. A signed deal is only the beginning — this stage carries a closed relationship from Stage 16.3 through a full simulated partnership term: activation, measurement, reporting, an issue, and a renewal decision.

Before starting: review your Stage 16.3 negotiation outcomes. If at least one negotiation reached a genuine close, use that deal as this stage's subject. If neither did (a legitimate outcome, given Stage 16.3's requirement that at least one negotiation not end in a full win), construct one additional, plausible closed deal consistent with your Stage 16.3 prospects and Stage 16.2 packages, explicitly stating this as an assumption before proceeding — this mirrors the real professional discipline, taught since Phase 15, of proceeding on reasonable stated assumptions rather than waiting for information that isn't available.

Objectives

By the end of this stage, you will have:

  1. A complete Full-Year Activation Calendar (Phase 09, Lesson 9.2) for the closed deal, integrating the Channel-by-Channel Activation Plan with measurement checkpoints and a reporting cadence.
  2. A complete Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report (Phase 10, Lesson 10.1–10.2), using the Phase 02 Exposure/Experience/Proof structure, populated with realistic data consistent with the activation calendar.
  3. A simulated mid-term issue, documented through the full Issue Resolution Protocol (Acknowledge, Investigate, Resolve, Follow Up).
  4. A genuine Upsell Signal Audit for this specific client relationship, concluding either a specific, well-justified upsell recommendation or a well-reasoned "not yet."
  5. A specific, evidence-based renewal recommendation, built cumulatively from the simulated term's activation and reporting history — not asserted without support.

Deliverables Required

  • Full-Year Activation Calendar (Phase 09, Lesson 9.2 template)
  • Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report with renewal recommendation (Phase 10, Lesson 10.2 template)
  • Issue Resolution Log, all four A-I-R-F steps documented (Phase 10, Lesson 10.2 template)
  • Upsell Signal Audit with conclusion and reasoning (Phase 10, Lesson 10.2 template)
  • QBR Agenda for at least one simulated quarterly check-in during the term (Phase 10, Lesson 10.1 template)

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Confirm the closed deal this stage will follow (from Stage 16.3, or a clearly stated constructed assumption per the note above).
  2. Build the Full-Year Activation Calendar, mapping every asset in the closed package to a specific activation channel with concrete, countable deliverables (Phase 09).
  3. Draft one QBR Agenda for a simulated mid-term check-in, including a genuine strategic discussion point, not just a status recap (Phase 10).
  4. Introduce one realistic issue during the simulated term (e.g., underperforming hospitality utilization, a delayed content deliverable) and carry it through the full Issue Resolution Protocol.
  5. Build the Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report, populated with realistic data reflecting the activation calendar and the resolved issue.
  6. Complete the Upsell Signal Audit, reaching a genuine, evidence-based conclusion.
  7. Write the final renewal recommendation, explicitly citing the cumulative evidence (activation performance, QBR outcomes, the resolved issue, the upsell conclusion) that supports it.

Standards

This stage is graded against the combined rubrics of Phase 09 (Lessons 9.1–9.2) and Phase 10 (Lessons 10.1–10.2), applied at full standard. Carryover requirements from those phases' certifications apply here without exception:

  • Hospitality/VIP utilization must be specifically tracked in the Sponsor Performance Report, not omitted — this remains the most commonly overlooked metric and is graded accordingly.
  • The renewal recommendation must be evidence-based, explicitly traceable to specific data in the report. A generic "we recommend renewal" with no cited support does not meet this stage's bar.
  • The upsell conclusion must reflect a genuine signal (or genuine absence of one) — a reflexive "yes, upsell" or "no, don't" without real reasoning fails this stage's standard, consistent with Phase 10's certification requirement.

Manager Coaching Notes

Purpose of this stage in the employee's development: This stage tests whether the employee can sustain a relationship through a full simulated term, not just execute a single planning exercise — the same discipline tested in Phases 9 and 10 individually, now carried through one continuous, integrated narrative.

Discussion questions to ask in review:

  • "Walk me through the issue you introduced — why did you choose that specific problem, and how realistic is the resolution you documented?"
  • "Your renewal recommendation — if I challenged it, what's the single strongest piece of evidence you'd point to?"
  • "How did your Upsell Signal Audit conclusion connect back to what actually happened during the simulated term, rather than a general assumption about this client?"

Common employee mistakes to watch for:

  • An activation calendar or performance report that doesn't actually connect to the specific package sold in Stage 16.2/closed in Stage 16.3.
  • A renewal recommendation asserted without specific, cited evidence.
  • An issue and resolution that feel token or perfunctory rather than genuinely worked through the full A-I-R-F protocol.

Stage Checkpoint

  • Full-Year Activation Calendar complete, directly reflecting the specific closed package
  • QBR Agenda complete with a genuine strategic discussion point
  • Issue Resolution Log complete, all four A-I-R-F steps genuinely worked through
  • Quarterly Sponsor Performance Report complete, including hospitality/VIP utilization tracking (hard gate)
  • Upsell Signal Audit complete with genuine, evidence-based reasoning
  • Renewal recommendation complete and explicitly evidence-based (hard gate)
  • Manager sign-off recorded

Once this checkpoint is complete, proceed to Stage 16.5 — Strategic Capstone & Executive Defense (draft pending your review of this stage) — the final stage of the entire academy.
