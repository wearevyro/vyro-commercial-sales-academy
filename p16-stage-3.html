Stage 16.3 — Business Development & Sales Execution

Overview

This stage covers Capstone Responsibilities 5 through 8: identify 100 target companies, prepare outreach campaigns, conduct simulated discovery meetings, and negotiate simulated agreements. It maps directly to Phase 04 (Prospecting & Outreach, Discovery/Negotiation/Closing), Phase 05 (Marketing objectives and channel fit), and Phase 12 (Business Development systems).

Where Stages 16.1 and 16.2 built the product (inventory, valuation, packaging), this stage sells it. Every prospect, every outreach message, every simulated meeting should reference the specific Final Package Ladder Document built in Stage 16.2 — this is not generic prospecting practice, it is selling this specific university's specific packages to specific, real-feeling companies.

Objectives

By the end of this stage, you will have:

  1. A qualified list of 100 target companies (brand-side prospects), each scored using F.A.T.N. (Phase 04) and matched to a specific likely marketing objective (Phase 05) and the specific package tier from Stage 16.2 that best fits that objective.
  2. A representative outreach campaign of 20 cold emails and 10 LinkedIn messages, each referencing genuine, specific research and the exact package tier being pitched — not generic templates.
  3. At least three complete simulated discovery meetings, each producing a full Discovery Meeting Guide and written needs analysis (stated vs. underlying needs) per Phase 04's Discovery Question Ladder.
  4. At least two complete simulated negotiations, carried through real objection handling (A-C-R-C) and the Negotiation Value Ladder to a resolved outcome — won, lost with clear reasoning, or deferred with clear reasoning.
  5. A brief acquisition-channel breakdown of the 100-company list (Phase 12's Client Acquisition Funnel) — how many prospects plausibly originate from outbound prospecting versus inbound/referral/partnership channels.

Deliverables Required

  • 100-company Prospect Qualification list (Phase 04, Lesson 4.1 template — F.A.T.N. scored), each tagged with a Phase 05 objective and a Stage 16.2 package tier match
  • 20 cold emails and 10 LinkedIn messages (Phase 04, Lesson 4.1 templates)
  • Three Discovery Meeting Guides with written needs analyses (Phase 04, Lesson 4.2 template)
  • Two full Negotiation records — Objection Response Logs (A-C-R-C) and a documented Negotiation Value Ladder application, each reaching a stated resolution
  • Acquisition channel breakdown of the 100-company list (Phase 12 framework)

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Using Stage 16.2's Final Package Ladder Document, identify which marketing objectives (Phase 05) each tier most naturally serves.
  2. Research and qualify 100 target companies, scoring each against F.A.T.N. and matching each to the tier and objective it best fits — reject or deprioritize any company that fails qualification rather than padding the list to reach 100.
  3. Select 30 of the strongest-qualified prospects and draft real, specific outreach (20 cold emails, 10 LinkedIn messages), each referencing genuine research and the specific package being proposed.
  4. Simulate three full discovery meetings (role-played with a colleague, manager, or designated practice partner), running the full Discovery Question Ladder and producing a needs analysis for each.
  5. Simulate two full negotiations building directly on two of the discovery meetings — introduce realistic objections, apply A-C-R-C, and negotiate from the Value Ladder before any price movement, carrying each to a real, stated resolution.
  6. Tag the full 100-company list by likely acquisition channel and summarize the breakdown.

Standards

This stage is graded against the combined rubrics of Phase 04 (Lessons 4.1–4.2), Phase 05, and Phase 12, applied at full standard. Carryover requirements from those phases' certifications apply here without exception:

  • Every one of the 100 companies must show genuine, specific qualification reasoning — a company scored "qualified" without a real F.A.T.N. basis, or a list where quality visibly degrades in the back half, does not meet this stage's bar (the same standard applied in Phase 04's certification).
  • At least one of the two simulated negotiations should include a scenario where the objection is not fully resolved with a full win — a Capstone negotiation record that is uniformly successful reads as unrealistic and avoids the harder skill of a credible partial or deferred resolution.
  • No generic outreach templates — every cold email and LinkedIn message must reference real, specific research and the exact package tier being pitched.

Manager Coaching Notes

Purpose of this stage in the employee's development: This stage tests whether the employee can run the full sales cycle, from cold prospect to negotiated (or realistically not-yet-closed) outcome, entirely in service of the specific product built in Stage 16.2 — not as a generic exercise, but as genuine selling of a genuine package.

Discussion questions to ask in review:

  • "Pick five companies from your list of 100 at random — walk me through the specific signal and objective match for each."
  • "In your two negotiations, which one didn't fully close, and why did you choose to end it that way rather than force a win?"
  • "How does your acquisition channel breakdown reflect what you learned in Phase 12 about where VYRO's strongest prospects actually come from?"

Common employee mistakes to watch for:

  • Padding the 100-company list with weakly qualified prospects to hit the number.
  • Outreach and discovery work that doesn't actually reference the specific Stage 16.2 packages, reading as generic Phase 04 practice rather than genuine Capstone integration.
  • Both negotiations ending in a clean win, avoiding the harder and more realistic skill of a credible partial resolution.

Stage Checkpoint

  • 100-company Prospect Qualification list complete, F.A.T.N.-scored, objective- and tier-matched, with no quality drop-off across the list
  • 20 cold emails and 10 LinkedIn messages complete, all specific and non-generic (hard gate)
  • Three Discovery Meeting Guides with needs analyses complete
  • Two full negotiation records complete, at least one with a non-full-win resolution
  • Acquisition channel breakdown complete
  • Manager sign-off recorded

Once this checkpoint is complete, proceed to Stage 16.4 — Activation & Client Management (draft pending your review of this stage).
