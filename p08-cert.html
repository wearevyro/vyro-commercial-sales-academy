Phase 08 Certification

Covers Lesson 8.1 (Package Architecture & Tier Design) and Lesson 8.2 (Pricing, Building & Presenting the Five Packages). An employee cannot begin Phase 09 until they pass this certification.

Phase 08 is the convergence point of everything taught in Phases 02 through 07 — terminology, assets, marketing objectives, and valuation all synthesize into one artifact: a complete, priced, defensible package ladder. This certification is built to test the whole system at once, not any single piece of it in isolation. An employee who can recite every framework from Phases 02–07 individually but cannot produce one coherent, sellable ladder under real conditions has not yet mastered this phase.

Knowledge Assessment

(Manager-administered, closed-notes, written. Time limit: 30 minutes. Answer key follows each question in italics — remove before administering.)

Section A — Recall (5 questions, 2 points each = 10 points)

  1. What is the Ladder Principle Test? Whether a brand could explain, in one sentence, why a higher tier is worth more than the one below it.

  2. State the Bundle Pricing Formula. Sum of individual asset valuations, minus a bundle discount, plus a separately quantified exclusivity premium where applicable.

  3. Why should scarce/exclusive assets be reserved for the top of the ladder? Including them at lower tiers cannibalizes the top tier's core differentiator and undermines the incentive to pay for the premium tier.

  4. Which objective from Phase 05 requires a real activation mechanism regardless of package tier? Lead Generation / Customer Acquisition.

  5. Why anchor a live presentation with the top tier before moving to the recommended tier? It establishes the ladder's overall value ceiling, making the tier the brand ultimately selects feel like stronger relative value.

Section B — Applied Package Build (30 points)

  1. Manager provides an unfamiliar organization profile and a summary of its top 20 highest-value assets (drawn from a hypothetical Phase 06/07 audit). In writing, design a complete five-tier ladder sketch (Lesson 8.1), price all five tiers using the Bundle Pricing Formula (Lesson 8.2), and write one business objective statement and one specific activation idea per tier. Graded against Lesson 8.1 §20 and Lesson 8.2 §20 rubric criteria across Business Understanding, Commercial Strategy, and Accuracy.

Section C — Objective Adaptation (20 points)

  1. Manager assigns a specific brand objective (from Phase 05's nine). Identify which tier from your Section 6 ladder best fits this objective, and rewrite that tier's business objective statement and activation idea specifically for a brand pursuing it. Graded on whether the adapted framing is genuinely specific to the assigned objective, not a generic restatement.

Total: 60 points. Minimum passing score for Knowledge Assessment: 48/60 (80%).

Practical Assessment

Exercise: Full Ladder Pitch & Pricing Defense

Manager assigns one unfamiliar, real or realistic organization, along with a summary asset list and a specific target brand objective (not disclosed until the build phase begins).

Time limit: 60 minutes to build, followed by a 20-minute live pitch and defense.

Deliverables (submitted before the live pitch):

  1. A complete Five-Tier Ladder Sketch (Lesson 8.1), passing the Ladder Principle Test at every adjacent pair.
  2. A complete Final Package Ladder Document (Lesson 8.2) — all five tiers priced, with assets, activation ideas, and business objective statements.

Live pitch structure:

  1. Presentation (10 minutes) — employee presents the full ladder to the manager, who plays a brand contact with the assigned objective, using strategic anchoring.
  2. Pricing defense (5 minutes) — manager challenges the pricing math on at least one tier, requiring a transparent walkthrough of the Bundle Pricing Formula.
  3. Differentiation defense (5 minutes) — manager picks two adjacent tiers and asks the employee to justify the price step-up between them, live.

Evaluation focus: whether the ladder holds together as one coherent system, whether the presentation correctly emphasizes the tier(s) most relevant to the assigned objective, and whether pricing and tier differentiation can both be defended transparently under direct questioning.

Manager Evaluation

Evaluation criteria:

  • Employee's homework ladder (Lessons 8.1 + 8.2) passes the Ladder Principle Test at every tier pair, with genuine differentiation throughout
  • All five tiers are priced using the full Bundle Pricing Formula, not a raw or arbitrary sum
  • Every tier includes a specific, non-generic activation idea and a client-ready business objective statement
  • Employee correctly identifies and adapts tier emphasis to a newly assigned brand objective in real time
  • Employee defends pricing transparently and specifically under live challenge, without vague reassurance
  • Employee defends tier-to-tier differentiation specifically and convincingly under live challenge
  • The final ladder reads as one coherent, genuinely client-ready product — not five disconnected proposals

Minimum Passing Score

Certification requires all three of the following:

Component Weight Minimum to Pass
Knowledge Assessment 30% 48/60 (80%)
Practical Assessment — Full Ladder Pitch & Pricing Defense (manager-scored against Lesson 8.1 §20 and Lesson 8.2 §20 rubrics, averaged) 50% 80/100 (80%)
Manager Evaluation 20% All 7 criteria checked, no unresolved concerns

The Practical Assessment carries the heaviest weight of any certification so far (50%, tied with Phase 04's Full Cycle Simulation), because Phase 08's entire purpose is producing one real, usable, defensible artifact — and that artifact either holds up under live scrutiny or it doesn't. As in every prior phase, failing any single component blocks advancement regardless of composite average.

Remediation path: Standard remediation (48 hours, unlimited attempts, role-fit conversation after a third failure) applies. For the Practical Assessment, remediation must include rebuilding at least one full tier pair from scratch and re-defending it live — not simply revising written notes — since the certified skill is real-time defensibility, not document quality alone.

Certification Checklist

  • Lesson 8.1 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Five-Tier Ladder Sketch + Ladder Principle Stress Test + asset allocation logic explanation)
  • Lesson 8.1 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 8.1 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Lesson 8.2 homework submitted and scored ≥75/100 (Activation Idea Bank + finalized five packages + Final Package Ladder Document)
  • Lesson 8.2 quiz passed (≥4/5)
  • Lesson 8.2 practical exercise and role-play completed
  • Final Package Ladder Document verified as genuinely client-ready (manager spot-check against real-proposal standard)
  • Phase 08 Knowledge Assessment passed (≥48/60)
  • Phase 08 Practical Assessment — Full Ladder Pitch & Pricing Defense — passed (≥80/100 average)
  • Phase 08 Manager Evaluation completed with all criteria checked
  • Manager sign-off recorded with date

Once every box is checked, the employee is certified in Phase 08 and cleared to begin Phase 09 — Partnership Activation.
